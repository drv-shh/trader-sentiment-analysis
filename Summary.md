Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment - Summary

Objective:
This project analyzes how market sentiment like fear vs greed influences trader behaviour and performance. The goal is to identify patterns that can inform better trading strategies.


Analysis:
1. Performance differs between fear and greed days as fear markets generate higher profits (avg PnL 7544.17), while greed markets show higher win rates( avg win rate 0.867) but lower overall returns (avg PnL 4799.4), indicating inefficient trading.
2. Yes, traders become more aggressive during fear periods with higher activity and larger positions (avg trades 133, long ratio 0.53, trade size 8975.9) while greed leads to reduced participation and smaller trades (avg trades 77, long ratio 0.47, trade size 6427.8).
3. High risk traders have highest PnL during Fear (15212) & perform best in volatile conditions. Whereas low risk traders have lowest PnL during Extreme fear (-790) and perform poor in volatile conditions.
4. Consistent winners (having high win rate) do not necessarily generate higher profits, highlighting the importance of trade size and risk-reward balance.


Key insights from Charts:
• Traders achieve the higher profits during fear periods despite higher risk.
• Greed periods produce higher win rates but lower overall profitability.
• High risk traders significantly outperform in volatile conditions. Low risk traders fail during extreme volatility.


Actionable Strategies:
Strategy 1: During Greed periods, limit trading activity and enforce stricter risk and reward thresholds to prevent inefficient trades.
Strategy 2: During fear periods, allow higher position sizing for high performing traders to capitalize on volatility.
Strategy 3: Avoid deploying low risk traders during Extreme fear conditions, as they consistently underperform.


Conclusion:
Market sentiment significantly impacts both trader behaviour and outcomes. Contrary to intuition, traders perform better during Fear than Greed, primarily due to more effective risk-taking. Segment-based strategies can further optimize performance by aligning trader profiles with market conditions.
